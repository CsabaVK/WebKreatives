# OpenClaw LinkedIn Automation Guidelines

Created: 2026-05-18

Purpose: canonical guideline for OpenClaw automated LinkedIn posting as WebKreatives.

Use this file whenever OpenClaw drafts, schedules, reviews, or publishes LinkedIn posts for WebKreatives.

## Main Goal

Every LinkedIn post should attract possible customers by being useful first.

A good WebKreatives post should:
- help the reader understand a website, SEO, trust, conversion, branding, or online-presence problem;
- offer a practical tip, checklist, statistic, diagnosis, or short explanation;
- lead to a specific WebKreatives article when the post is article-led;
- make WebKreatives feel credible, helpful, and relevant without sounding desperate.

The post should make a potential client think:

"This is exactly the problem with my website. I should talk to WebKreatives."

## Brand Position

WebKreatives is a custom web design and development studio for brands that have outgrown templates.

Core message:
- Custom websites, not template swaps.
- Built around the client's brand, offer, audience, goals, and budget.
- AI-assisted workflows for faster research, planning, writing, design exploration, and iteration.
- Human judgment, practical launch quality, and support after launch.
- International client direction, English-first LinkedIn communication.

Avoid positioning WebKreatives as:
- a cheap website shop;
- a generic AI agency;
- a template reseller;
- a traditional slow agency;
- a "we can build literally anything" hype brand.

## Research Principles

Automated posting should follow these research-backed principles:

- LinkedIn company pages need clear category language and searchable service terms.
- Educational posts are better for trust than direct sales posts.
- Article posts should give value inside LinkedIn, not only tease the click.
- External links are acceptable when the post itself is useful and the link is exact.
- Statistics must be verified before use.
- Consistent posting matters more than high volume.

## Posting Cadence

Default cadence:
- 2 LinkedIn posts per week minimum.
- 3 LinkedIn posts per week ideal while building awareness.
- Do not post more than once per day unless there is a specific reason.

Recommended rhythm:
- Tuesday: educational article post.
- Thursday: checklist, audit, or practical warning post.
- Optional Monday or Saturday: founder/studio point-of-view post.

Do not post late at night unless performance data later proves it works.

## Post Objectives

Each post must choose one primary objective.

### Diagnose A Problem

Use when the article or post explains what is hurting the business.

Examples:
- "Your homepage may be losing trust before people contact you."
- "A one-page website can be too small for a serious offer."
- "A generic website makes a serious business look interchangeable."

### Teach A Concept

Use when the post explains how something works.

Examples:
- local SEO basics;
- what a homepage must answer;
- why reviews help trust;
- what technical SEO means in plain English.

### Share A Statistic

Use when a credible source supports the point.

Rules:
- Do not invent numbers.
- Use current sources.
- Mention the source name in the post if the statistic is specific.
- If the article does not already contain the statistic/source, update the article before posting.

### Offer A Checklist

Use when the reader can apply the post immediately.

Examples:
- homepage audit;
- website quote checklist;
- local SEO basics;
- redesign warning signs.

### Start A Service Conversation

Use sparingly.

The CTA should be soft:
- "If this sounds familiar, it may be time to review your website."
- "Need a second opinion on your website? WebKreatives can help."
- "If your site feels generic, this article is a good starting point."

Avoid hard-sales language.

## Article-Led Posting Rules

Most WebKreatives LinkedIn posts should highlight a WebKreatives article.

For article-led posts:
- Always link the specific article.
- Never link only the homepage when an article exists.
- Never link only the articles index when a specific article is being discussed.
- The post must be useful even if the reader does not click.
- The article link should be placed clearly near the end.

Correct:

`https://webkreatives.com/articles/local-seo-how-google-finds-you-in-your-city/`

Wrong:

`https://webkreatives.com/`

Preferred tracking format:

`?utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=wk_articles&utm_content=<article-slug>`

Example:

`https://webkreatives.com/articles/local-seo-how-google-finds-you-in-your-city/?utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=social&utm_campaign=wk_articles&utm_content=local-seo-how-google-finds-you-in-your-city`

## Post Structure

Default structure:

1. Hook: one clear line naming the problem or insight.
2. Context: why it matters for a business.
3. Value: 3-5 bullets, checklist items, warning signs, or practical observations.
4. Bridge: connect the idea to the article.
5. Exact article link.
6. Soft CTA or question.
7. 3-5 relevant hashtags.

Recommended length:
- 700-1300 characters for most posts.
- Up to 1800 characters for strong checklist posts.
- First 140 characters must be clear before "see more."

## Media Rules

Good media:
- article Open Graph image;
- screenshot of article hero;
- before/after website section;
- simple visual checklist;
- relevant portfolio screenshot.

Avoid:
- generic stock photos;
- random AI art;
- decorative graphics with no information.

Do not block posting if no media is available, but prefer a visual when the article has a good preview image.

## Automation Workflow

For every automated post, OpenClaw should:

1. Select one article or content angle.
2. Extract title, canonical URL, meta description, publication date, and topic.
3. Choose one objective.
4. Choose one post type.
5. Draft the post.
6. Verify the exact article URL opens.
7. Verify any statistic is sourced.
8. Add 3-5 relevant hashtags.
9. Save a draft/log entry before publishing.
10. Publish only if public posting permission exists.
11. Record published URL, article URL, date/time, angle, and performance notes.

## Quality Gate

Before publishing, all answers must be yes:

- Is this useful without clicking?
- Does it link the exact article?
- Is the article URL working?
- Is the first line clear?
- Does it avoid hype?
- Does it connect to a real customer problem?
- Is WebKreatives present but not pushy?
- Are hashtags relevant and limited?
- If there is a statistic, is it sourced?
- Has this article avoided overuse in the last 30 days?

If any answer is no, save as draft instead of publishing.

## Follow-Up

After posting:
- check comments within 24 hours;
- reply naturally and helpfully;
- invite a DM only when the person has a real website/project question;
- save good questions as future article ideas;
- track clicks and engagement when analytics are available.

## Public Action Safety

Posting to LinkedIn is a public external action.

OpenClaw must not publish automatically unless the current automation flow has explicit permission to post publicly. Without permission, create a draft and report it.
